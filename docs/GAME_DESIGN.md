# Game Design

## Player fantasy

The player is a portfolio architect, not a button-clicking speculator. Success requires building a thesis, understanding regime transmission, controlling execution costs, preserving capital, and outperforming a benchmark and rival funds.

## Modes

### Academy
Explanations are prominent. The player studies regimes, first- and second-order effects, risk metrics, and order behavior.

### Career
The same deterministic engine is framed as a competitive fund-management run. Rival styles expose different failure modes: trend crowding, macro timing risk, and crypto concentration.

## Regimes

- AI boom
- Sticky inflation
- Rate-cut rally
- Credit stress
- Geopolitical supply shock
- Crypto risk-on

Each asset has explicit regime and macro sensitivities. Regimes are not simple global multipliers; they interact with growth, inflation, rates, credit spreads, energy pressure, and crypto liquidity.

## Information design

The game separates headline narrative from audit evidence. Every tick records component contributions for every asset, allowing the player to inspect whether price moves came from regime, macro, direct news, second-order effects, or idiosyncratic flow.
